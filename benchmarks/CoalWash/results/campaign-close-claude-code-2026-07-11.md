## What passing 33 classes did NOT make safe — the three standing cautions

The traps were caught; these are the failure modes the campaign proved are REAL and live on into real use (each earned from a live lab incident):

1. **The OPERATOR is the primary failure surface, not the model.** The sharpest loss of the campaign (T71) came from a hand-composed role contract that DROPPED a standing protection line — the model then misjudged exactly the class the dropped line guarded. Fix graduated: standing TEMPLATE-ONLY contracts for every lab role, placeholders filled, never re-composed. Consequence for real use: the skill is safe only as far as the orchestrating agent does not let a contract/protection drift between runs — the risk moved from the engine to the operator.
2. **A "dry" verdict is sampling- AND angle-relative — never trust it raw.** M17 evaded sampled probes for two rounds; round 6's dry verdict was falsified by round 7 the moment the hunt used a varied lens (7 findings six same-angle passes never saw). The retirement protocol now requires a FULL sweep (no sampling) plus one varied-angle pass before closing — but the caution stands: coverage is a union over angles, not repetitions of one.
3. **Recovery is not prevention — a caught defect must be REVERTED, not just recorded.** ADD-01 was caught at round 2, its protection written the same round — yet the contamination itself sat in the corpus for five more rounds because documenting a catch is not undoing the damage. Rule: a caught addition/loss is reverted in the same edit, then the protection is written.

These are why the beta→rc→live ladder holds a dogfood gate: the lab proved the floor is code-held; only real use pressures the operator-drift, angle-coverage, and revert-discipline surfaces the lab can only simulate.
